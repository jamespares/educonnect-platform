VPN & Internet Setup (Critical!)

Set Up Before You Arrive

Install and test your VPN while you're still outside China. Many VPN websites are blocked once you arrive, making it difficult to set up later.

China blocks many Western websites and services including Google, Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, WhatsApp, and Reddit. A reliable VPN is essential for accessing these services and staying connected with home.

Police Registration (Mandatory)

24-Hour Rule

You must register with local police within 24 hours of arriving at any address in China. This applies to hotels, apartments, and even when returning from international travel.

Police registration is a legal requirement that many newcomers overlook. Hotels handle this automatically, but if you move into an apartment or change addresses, you must register personally.

Required Documents

  • Valid passport with current visa/residence permit
  • Rental contract (lease agreement) with landlord details
  • Landlord's property certificate (Êàø‰∫ßËØÅ) - landlord should provide
  • Landlord's ID copy (if registering alone)

Registration Process

  1. Find your local PSB: Locate the nearest Public Security Bureau office
  2. Fill out forms: Complete registration form (English versions available in major cities)
  3. Get receipt: Receive registration form - keep this safe with your passport
  4. Time needed: Usually 15-30 minutes if all documents are correct
Important Notes
  • Re-register every time you change addresses
  • Re-register when returning from international travel
  • Keep the registration form safe - you'll need it for visa renewals
  • Some landlords can help with the registration process

Finding Housing & Neighborhoods

Understanding the Rental Process

3+1 Payment Structure

Standard rental payment in China requires 3 months rent upfront + 1 month security deposit + agent fees. Budget for 4.5-5 months of rent initially.

Typical Costs Breakdown

  • 3 months rent in advance - Standard payment period
  • 1 month security deposit - Refundable if no damage
  • Agent commission - Usually 0.5-1 month rent (sometimes split with landlord)
  • Total upfront cost: 4.5-5 months of rent
  • Monthly payment option: Available through some platforms at higher rates

Required Documents for Renting

Heating in Southern China

Cities south of the Yangtze River (including Shanghai, Hangzhou, Chengdu) don't have central heating. You'll rely on AC units for heating in winter, which can significantly increase electricity costs.

Legal Requirements & Work Permits

Understanding China's legal requirements is crucial for staying compliant and avoiding serious problems. Always work legally and keep your documentation in order.

Work Visa & Residence Permit Process

Only Work Legally

Never work on a tourist or business visa. It's illegal and can result in detention, deportation, and being banned from re-entering China. Legitimate employers will handle proper work permit applications.

Proper Work Authorization Process

  1. Z Visa Entry: Enter China on a proper work visa (Z visa)
  2. Residence Permit: Convert Z visa to residence permit within 30 days
  3. Work Permit: Obtain work permit before starting employment
  4. Annual Renewal: Keep permits current and renewed on time

Required Documents for Work Permit

  • Educational Qualifications: Bachelor's degree minimum (authenticated and translated)
  • Criminal Background Check: From your home country (authenticated)
  • Medical Examination: Completed in China at approved facilities
  • Employment Contract: With a licensed Chinese employer
  • Work Experience: Proof of relevant work experience (2+ years preferred)

Important Legal Compliance

What You Must Do

  • Police registration: Within 24 hours of any address change
  • Visa compliance: Never overstay or work illegally
  • Tax obligations: Pay income tax on Chinese earnings
  • Permit renewals: Keep all permits current
  • Document carrying: Always have passport and permits accessible

What You Must Avoid

  • Drug-related activities: Extremely strict laws with severe penalties
  • Illegal employment: Working without proper permits
  • Visa violations: Overstaying or working on wrong visa type
  • Political activities: Avoid political demonstrations or sensitive discussions
  • Prostitution: Strictly illegal with harsh penalties

Navigating Sensitive Topics

As a foreigner in China, it's important to be aware of sensitive political and social topics. While you won't get in trouble for casual conversations, it's wise to be diplomatic.

Topics to Approach Carefully

  • The "Three T's": Tibet, Taiwan, Tiananmen Square
  • Xinjiang and human rights issues
  • Hong Kong protests and politics
  • Chinese government criticism
  • Falun Gong and banned religious groups
  • Comparison of political systems
Best Approach

Focus on cultural exchange rather than political debates. If asked sensitive questions, you can politely say "I don't know enough about that topic" or redirect to cultural differences instead.

Safe Topics for Discussion

  • Cultural differences: Food, customs, holidays
  • Travel and tourism: Places you've visited in China
  • Language learning: Your experience learning Chinese
  • Sports and entertainment: Movies, music, sports
  • Technology: Apps, gadgets, innovation
  • Education: Teaching methods, student differences

Final Tips for Success

Living in China will be one of the most transformative experiences of your life. Here are final pieces of advice to help you not just survive, but truly thrive.

Mindset & Attitude

Stay Flexible and Positive

The first few months will be a rollercoaster of emotions. Expect culture shock after the initial honeymoon period. This is completely normal and temporary.

Mental Preparation

  • Embrace the chaos: Things won't always go as planned - roll with it
  • Maintain sense of humor: Laugh at misunderstandings and mistakes
  • Stay curious: Approach differences with genuine interest
  • Be patient: Everything takes longer when you're learning
  • Celebrate small wins: Successfully ordering food in Chinese is an achievement!

Dealing with Homesickness

  • Maintain connections home: Regular video calls with family
  • Create routines: Weekly activities that give structure
  • Find comfort foods: Import stores or learn to cook favorites
  • Join expat support groups: Others understand what you're going through
  • Focus on growth: Remember why you came and what you're gaining

Learning & Growth

Language Learning Strategy

  • Take it seriously: Dedicate 1-2 hours daily to study
  • Use it daily: Practice with taxi drivers, shopkeepers, neighbors
  • Focus on tones: Critical for being understood
  • Learn practical phrases: Daily life vocabulary before literature
  • Find language partners: Chinese friends learning English
  • Use apps effectively: Pleco for dictionary, HelloChinese for structure

Cultural Integration Tips

  • Say yes to invitations: Even if it's outside your comfort zone
  • Learn chopstick etiquette: Don't point or stick them upright in rice
  • Practice gift-giving culture: Use both hands, modest protests when receiving
  • Understand "face" concept: Help others maintain dignity and respect
  • Join local activities: Tai chi in parks, mahjong games, calligraphy classes

Health & Wellness

Physical Health

  • Bring medications: Especially prescription and specialty items
  • Get health insurance: Understand what your school provides
  • Find international clinics: For English-speaking doctors
  • Air quality awareness: Use AQI apps, air purifiers, masks when needed
  • Stay hydrated: Adopt the Chinese habit of drinking warm water

Mental Health

  • Maintain exercise routine: Join gyms, sports groups, running clubs
  • Practice stress management: Meditation, journaling, hobbies
  • Stay connected socially: Don't isolate when things get tough
  • Seek help when needed: Online counseling, expat support groups
  • Take breaks: Travel home or to nearby countries when overwhelmed

Financial Success

Saving Money Tips

  • Live like locals: Use local restaurants, public transport, wet markets
  • Avoid expat premiums: Don't always shop at international stores
  • Use technology: Delivery apps, group buying, seasonal sales
  • Learn to bargain: Markets and small shops expect negotiation
  • Cook at home occasionally: Even basic Chinese dishes

Building for the Future

  • Send money home regularly: Use efficient services like Panda Remit
  • Keep good financial records: For taxes and future visa applications
  • Build Chinese credit history: Consistent use of WeChat/Alipay
  • Network professionally: Connections can lead to better opportunities
  • Develop marketable skills: Chinese language, cross-cultural communication

Making the Most of Your Experience

Travel & Exploration

  • Explore domestically: China is incredibly diverse - see different regions
  • Use holiday time wisely: Teachers get excellent vacation periods
  • Visit neighboring countries: Southeast Asia, Japan, Korea are accessible
  • Try high-speed rail: Efficient, comfortable way to see the country
  • Document your journey: Photos, journal, blog for memories

Professional Development

  • Get teaching certifications: TEFL, TESOL, subject-specific qualifications
  • Learn about Chinese education: Different pedagogical approaches
  • Network internationally: Connect with other expat teachers
  • Develop leadership skills: Take on extra responsibilities
  • Build a portfolio: Document your achievements and growth
Remember Your "Why"

On difficult days, remember why you chose this adventure. Whether it's professional growth, cultural immersion, financial goals, or personal challenge - keep your original motivation in mind.

Preparing for Success

China will challenge you, change you, and ultimately reward you with experiences and perspectives you could never gain anywhere else. The key to success is approaching everything with an open mind, patience, and sense of adventure.

You're Ready for This Adventure

Thousands of teachers have successfully made this transition before you. With proper preparation, the right attitude, and willingness to embrace the experience, you'll not only survive but thrive in China. Welcome to what may become the most rewarding chapter of your life!
