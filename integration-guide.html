Getting Started - Your First Week

VPN & Internet Setup (Critical!)

Set Up Before You Arrive

Install and test your VPN while you're still outside China. Many VPN websites are blocked once you arrive, making it difficult to set up later.

China blocks many Western websites and services including Google, Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, WhatsApp, and Reddit. A reliable VPN is essential for accessing these services and staying connected with home.

Digital Privacy & Security

Living in China's digital ecosystem requires understanding privacy implications and taking appropriate security measures to protect your personal information and data.

VPN Legal Considerations

VPN Legal Reality

VPNs exist in a legal gray area in China. While commonly used by expats and businesses, technically only government-approved VPNs are legal. Use discretion and understand the risks.

  • Business use: Companies often have approved VPN access for international operations
  • Personal use: Widely tolerated but technically restricted
  • Avoid discussion: Don't openly discuss VPN usage in public or online
  • Keep low profile: Use VPNs discretely, avoid streaming during sensitive periods
  • Have alternatives: Multiple VPN services and communication methods

Data Privacy & Surveillance Awareness

  • WeChat monitoring: All messages and activities are potentially monitored
  • Avoid sensitive topics: Political discussions, criticism of government policies
  • Location tracking: Apps track location for services but also for monitoring
  • Payment data: All mobile payment transactions are recorded and traceable
  • Social credit implications: Online behavior may affect social credit scores

Safe Communication Practices

Cybersecurity Best Practices

  • Use strong passwords: Unique passwords for each account, password manager recommended
  • Two-factor authentication: Enable 2FA on all important accounts
  • Regular updates: Keep apps and operating systems updated
  • Public WiFi caution: Avoid sensitive activities on public networks
  • Backup data regularly: Cloud backup to multiple services
  • Antivirus software: Use reputable antivirus on devices
Digital Hygiene Tips
  • Regularly review and clean app permissions
  • Log out of banking apps after each use
  • Use biometric locks on devices
  • Be cautious with public charging stations
  • Separate personal and work digital activities where possible

Cloud Storage & Data Management

  • Multiple cloud services: Don't rely on just one provider
  • International cloud services: Google Drive, Dropbox, iCloud (via VPN)
  • Chinese cloud services: Baidu Cloud, Tencent Cloud for local needs
  • Document encryption: Encrypt sensitive documents before cloud storage
  • Regular backups: Photos, important documents, contacts

Financial App Security

  • Banking apps: Always log out completely after use
  • WeChat/Alipay security: Enable face/fingerprint recognition
  • Transaction limits: Set daily/monthly limits for mobile payments
  • Monitor statements: Regularly check for unauthorized transactions
  • Separate devices: Consider separate device for financial apps if very security-conscious
Balanced Approach to Digital Life

Don't let security concerns prevent you from enjoying China's digital convenience. Use common sense: be cautious with sensitive information, but don't be paranoid about daily activities like ordering food or chatting with friends.

Police Registration (Mandatory)

24-Hour Rule

You must register with local police within 24 hours of arriving at any address in China. This applies to hotels, apartments, and even when returning from international travel.

Police registration is a legal requirement that many newcomers overlook. Hotels handle this automatically, but if you move into an apartment or change addresses, you must register personally.

Required Documents

  • Valid passport with current visa/residence permit
  • Rental contract (lease agreement) with landlord details
  • Landlord's property certificate (Êàø‰∫ßËØÅ) - landlord should provide
  • Landlord's ID copy (if registering alone)

Registration Process

  1. Find your local PSB: Locate the nearest Public Security Bureau office
  2. Fill out forms: Complete registration form (English versions available in major cities)
  3. Get receipt: Receive registration form - keep this safe with your passport
  4. Time needed: Usually 15-30 minutes if all documents are correct
Important Notes
  • Re-register every time you change addresses
  • Re-register when returning from international travel
  • Keep the registration form safe - you'll need it for visa renewals
  • Some landlords can help with the registration process

Advanced Financial Planning

Tax Obligations & Treaties

Understanding your tax obligations in both China and your home country is crucial for long-term financial health.

  • Chinese income tax: Progressive rates from 3% to 45% on Chinese-source income
  • Tax treaties: Many countries have agreements to avoid double taxation
  • Home country obligations: Americans must file taxes globally; others may have different rules
  • Professional help: Consider tax advisors familiar with expat situations
  • Tax residence: Usually determined by 183+ days in China per year
Important Tax Considerations
  • Keep detailed records of income, expenses, and taxes paid in China
  • Save all fapiaos for potential tax deductions
  • Understand exit tax implications if leaving China
  • Some countries require foreign bank account reporting

Tax Optimization Strategies

Understanding tax residency rules and planning your time strategically can lead to significant tax savings, especially for those with multiple income sources.

The 183-Day Rule

Most countries, including China, determine tax residency based on spending 183+ days per year in the country. Strategic travel can help optimize your tax obligations across multiple jurisdictions.

Strategic Tax Residency Management

  • Track days carefully: Use apps or spreadsheets to monitor time in each country
  • Plan extended trips: 2-3 month trips abroad can help manage residency status
  • School holidays advantage: Teachers can use long holidays for strategic travel
  • Avoid partial year complications: Understand entry/exit year tax implications
  • Treaty benefits: Leverage tax treaties between countries for optimization

Understanding the Rental Process

3+1 Payment Structure

Standard rental payment in China requires 3 months rent upfront + 1 month security deposit + agent fees. Budget for 4.5-5 months of rent initially.

Typical Costs Breakdown

  • 3 months rent in advance - Standard payment period
  • 1 month security deposit - Refundable if no damage
  • Agent commission - Usually 0.5-1 month rent (sometimes split with landlord)
  • Total upfront cost: 4.5-5 months of rent
  • Monthly payment option: Available through some platforms at higher rates

Required Documents for Renting

Learning to Drive in China

Getting a Chinese driver's license can be challenging but rewarding. The process requires patience, study, and often an English-speaking instructor to navigate the requirements successfully.

Who Can Drive in China

Foreigners with valid residence permits can obtain Chinese driver's licenses. Tourist visa holders cannot get licenses but may drive with International Driving Permits for short periods (rules vary by city).

Driver's License Requirements

  • Residence permit: Valid work or residence permit required
  • Medical certificate: Health check from approved hospital
  • Passport photos: Recent white background photos
  • Previous license: Your home country license (if applying for transfer)
  • Age requirements: 18+ for cars, different ages for motorcycles

License Application Process

  1. Medical examination: Vision, hearing, and physical health check at designated hospital (¬•30-50)
  2. Driving school registration: Choose a school with English-speaking instructors
  3. Theory test (ÁßëÁõÆ‰∏Ä): Traffic rules and regulations (100 questions, need 90+ to pass)

Legal Requirements & Work Permits

Understanding China's legal requirements is crucial for staying compliant and avoiding serious problems. Always work legally and keep your documentation in order.

Work Visa & Residence Permit Process

Only Work Legally

Never work on a tourist or business visa. It's illegal and can result in detention, deportation, and being banned from re-entering China. Legitimate employers will handle proper work permit applications.

Proper Work Authorization Process

  1. Z Visa Entry: Enter China on a proper work visa (Z visa)
  2. Residence Permit: Convert Z visa to residence permit within 30 days
  3. Work Permit: Obtain work permit before starting employment
  4. Annual Renewal: Keep permits current and renewed on time

Required Documents for Work Permit

  • Educational Qualifications: Bachelor's degree minimum (authenticated and translated)
  • Criminal Background Check: From your home country (authenticated)
  • Medical Examination: Completed in China at approved facilities
  • Employment Contract: With a licensed Chinese employer
  • Work Experience: Proof of relevant work experience (2+ years preferred)

Important Legal Compliance

What You Must Do

  • Police registration: Within 24 hours of any address change
  • Visa compliance: Never overstay or work illegally
  • Tax obligations: Pay income tax on Chinese earnings
  • Permit renewals: Keep all permits current
  • Document carrying: Always have passport and permits accessible

What You Must Avoid

  • Drug-related activities: Extremely strict laws with severe penalties
  • Illegal employment: Working without proper permits
  • Visa violations: Overstaying or working on wrong visa type
  • Political activities: Avoid political demonstrations or sensitive discussions
  • Prostitution: Strictly illegal with harsh penalties

Seasonal Living & Climate Adaptation

China's vast territory means dramatically different climates across regions and seasons. Understanding and preparing for these changes is essential for comfort and health.

Air Quality & Pollution Management
  • Air purifiers: Essential for bedrooms, especially in northern cities
  • Masks: N95 masks for heavily polluted days (AQI >150)
  • Apps: AirVisual, AQICN for real-time air quality monitoring
  • Indoor plants: Spider plants, peace lilies help purify indoor air
  • Exercise timing: Avoid outdoor activities during high pollution days

Seasonal Food & Nutrition

  • Winter warmth: Hot pot, soups, warm beverages (following local customs)
  • Summer cooling: Cold noodles, fresh fruits, coconut water, green tea
  • Seasonal vegetables: Embrace local seasonal produce for best prices and freshness
  • Hydration: Carry water bottles, warm water in winter (Chinese custom)
  • Food safety: More careful with street food in summer heat

Dealing with Extreme Weather

  • Typhoons (Southeast Coast): Stock food/water, avoid travel, follow government alerts
  • Winter heating bills: Southern China heating costs can be shocking - budget accordingly
  • Summer power usage: AC costs can be high, consider energy-efficient settings
  • Rainy season: Waterproof shoes, umbrella always, mold prevention
  • Sandstorms (North): Goggles, masks, indoor air filtration

Daily Life Optimization

Time-Saving Life Hacks

  • Delivery everything: Food, groceries, medicine, even McDonald's - save time and energy
  • WeChat mini-programs: Pay utilities, book services, order taxis all in one app
  • Avoid rush hours: 8-9am, 6-7pm are packed - plan accordingly
  • Meal prep Chinese style: Buy large portions, use delivery apps strategically
  • Metro passes: Monthly passes save money and time vs individual tickets

Cost-Cutting Strategies

  • Shop local markets: Wet markets 30-50% cheaper than supermarkets
  • Group buying: WeChat group buys for bulk discounts
  • Seasonal sales: 11.11, 6.18, Chinese New Year for major purchases
  • Negotiate rent: Annual payments often get discounts
  • Local vs expat pricing: Learn to recognize and avoid foreigner pricing

Health & Wellness Routines

  • Social connections: Loneliness is the biggest threat to expat mental health
Practical Daily Essentials
  • Always carry: Tissues, hand sanitizer, phone charger, cash backup

  • Transportation: Comfortable walking shoes, metro card, DiDi app ready
