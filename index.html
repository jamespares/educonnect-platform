Success Stories

Hear from teachers who've transformed their careers

"Look, I'll be honest - the first few months were rough. Couldn't find decent tea anywhere and kept getting lost on the metro. But the school really backed me when I pitched some changes to the curriculum. Now I'm Head of Department, which would've taken me decades back home. Still miss proper Sunday roasts though."

Emma Richardson

Head of English, Shanghai

"Was supposed to be a gap year adventure - you know how it is. Four years later and I'm still here! Met my wife at a dumpling place near school (she laughed at my terrible chopstick skills). Teaching here's been wild - some days the kids drive me up the wall, but when they get excited about geography... it's worth it. Plus, where else can you visit the Great Wall on weekends?"

James Carter

Geography Teacher, Beijing

"Not gonna lie, the salary sold me. Been putting away more cash than I ever could at home - finally bought mum that house she wanted. The kids here are fitness mad which makes PE lessons a breeze, though explaining cricket rules in broken Mandarin was... interesting. Still can't handle the spicy food though - learned that the hard way!"

Tom Fletcher

PE Teacher, Shenzhen

"The tech here is insane compared to what we had back home. Had to Google half the equipment when I first arrived! The kids pick up coding faster than I can keep up sometimes - bit embarrassing really. Parents actually thank me at pickup though, which is... nice. Different from the complaints I used to get about homework being too hard."

Alex Murphy

Science Teacher, Guangzhou

"Proper scared when I landed - couldn't even order coffee without pointing and smiling like an idiot. EduConnect sorted the boring stuff while I figured out how to use chopsticks without stabbing myself. My Chinese is still rubbish but the kids find my attempts hilarious. At least I know how to ask for the toilet now... learned that one quick!"
